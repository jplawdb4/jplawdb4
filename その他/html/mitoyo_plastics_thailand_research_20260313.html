2. Products & Manufacturing

Mitoyo Plastics Thailand is engaged in the manufacture of resin (plastic) injection-molded products (樹脂射出成形製品の製造), primarily for the automotive industry.

Product Categories Manufactured at Thailand Factory

CategorySpecific Products
Automotive Structural Parts Base cases Blower units Grommets
Meter & Instrument Parts Meter parts (instrument cluster components)
Interior Trim Parts Interior trim components (with texturing, plating, painting capabilities)
Non-Automotive Products Inkjet printer components

Manufacturing Processes

  • Primary Process: Plastic injection molding (50-385 ton machines)
  • Secondary Processing:
    • Pad printing (tampon printing)
    • Hot stamping
    • Laser processing (marking/cutting)
    • Welding (ultrasonic, heat plate, vibration)
    • Surface treatment (texturing, plating, painting)
  • Materials: Various engineering plastics and resins (PP, ABS, PA, POM, etc. typical for automotive applications)

Broader Mitoyo Group Product Portfolio (for reference)

The parent Mitoyo group manufactures the following across all facilities, some of which may also be handled by the Thailand plastics operation in coordination with the rubber subsidiary:

Passenger Vehicles

  • Fuel hoses (high/low pressure)
  • Fuel lids/caps
  • Seat components (plastic moldings, dampers)
  • Intake system parts (air cleaners, ducts, sound creators)
  • Canister components (emissions control)
  • A/C hoses and pipes
  • Reserve tanks
  • Cabin filters

Trucks & Commercial Vehicles

  • Urea water tanks (AdBlue/DEF)
  • Mud flaps (embossed/two-color molded)
  • Seat bellows covers
  • Fuel hoses
  • A/C systems

EV-Specific

  • Coolant reserve tanks
  • A/C hose/pipe assemblies
  • Interior instrument panel parts

8. History & Timeline

December 1948 - Mitoyo Co., Ltd. established in Japan as an industrial rubber products trading company (original name: 三豊ゴム)
1965 - Mitoyo enters automotive rubber parts manufacturing (first in-house factory)
1969 - Begins hose/pipe production for vehicle air conditioners
1978 - Establishes plastics division
June 1987 - Company renamed from "三豊ゴム" to "株式会社ミトヨ" (Mitoyo Co., Ltd.)
1996 - Capital alliance with Unika Rubber Products Sdn. Bhd. (Johor Bahru, Malaysia) - first overseas expansion
August 2004 - Mitoyo (Thailand) Co., Ltd. established in Thailand (first Thai subsidiary)
2006 - Shanghai Mitoyo Sugie Trading Co., Ltd. established in China
December 2010 - Mitoyo Rubber (Thailand) Co., Ltd. established in Chonburi (Amatanakorn Industrial Estate)
April 2013 - Mitoyo Plastics (Thailand) Co., Ltd. established (registered April 30, 2013) in Chachoengsao
December 2013 - ISO 9001 certification obtained (Thailand Plastics)
May 2014 - ISO 14001 certification obtained (Thailand Plastics)
September 2016 - New factory building completed; relocation to current facility at 1/50 Moo 5, Tha Sa-an
September 2017 - Merger: Mitoyo (Thailand) Co., Ltd. (est. 2004) is integrated into Mitoyo Plastics (Thailand) Co., Ltd.
August 2018 - IATF 16949 certification obtained (both Thailand Plastics and Thailand Rubber)
June 2020 - Tetsushi Takeshima appointed President of Mitoyo Japan
February 2025 - Kuriyama Holdings announces acquisition of Mitoyo for JPY 6.1 billion
April 2, 2025 - Acquisition completed: Mitoyo becomes a grandchild company (孫会社) of Kuriyama Holdings via Kuriyama Japan
Context of the 2017 merger: The original Mitoyo (Thailand) Co., Ltd. (est. 2004) likely served as a trading/sales office for the initial Thai market entry. When the plastics manufacturing subsidiary was established in 2013 and grew, it made strategic sense to consolidate the older trading entity into the manufacturing entity, simplifying the corporate structure and reducing administrative overhead. This is a common pattern among Japanese manufacturers in Thailand.

Collaboration Model

According to the Bangkok Chamber of Commerce profile for Mitoyo Rubber Thailand, the two companies coordinate to achieve customer satisfaction regarding quality and cost efficiency. They operate as the "Mitoyo Thailand Group" (ミトヨタイランドグループ) and can jointly support customers requiring both plastic and rubber components.

  • Shared Managing Director: Both entities are led by Tetsuzo Yamakawa, ensuring unified management
  • Complementary capabilities: Plastics = injection molding; Rubber = compression/transfer molding with metal bonding
  • Geographic proximity: Chachoengsao and Chonburi are adjacent provinces in the Eastern Seaboard industrial zone, approximately 50-60 km apart
  • Combined offering: Many automotive assemblies require both rubber and plastic components (e.g., grommets with rubber seals, plastic housings with rubber gaskets), enabling one-stop sourcing
  • Mitoyo Rubber's proprietary compound formulations: Custom material development using in-house compound formulations for adhesion with metals or fabrics
