2. Business Details & Products

2.1 Core Business

Unika Rubber specializes in manufacturing precision rubber parts and metal-to-rubber bonding products for the automobile, electronics, and electrical industries. The company also produces custom rubber compounds.

2.2 Product Categories

Product TypeDetails
Rubber GasketsPrecision-molded gaskets for automotive and industrial sealing
Seal RingsAnnular seals for shafts, housings, and fluid systems
O-RingsStandard and custom O-rings in various compounds
Rubber ValvesRubber valve components for fluid control
Rubber DampersVibration-absorbing/dampening parts for automotive
Rubber StoppersPlugs and stoppers for various industrial applications
Rubber PlugsProtective plugs for holes, ports, and openings
GrommetsWiring pass-through protection in auto/electronics
Metal-to-Rubber Bonded PartsMetal inserts bonded with rubber for structural and sealing applications

2.4 Raw Material Sourcing

Rubber raw materials and polymers are imported from Japan, Europe, and the United States. This import-dependent sourcing is typical of a Japanese JV ensuring quality control through specified material grades.

2.5 Industry Sectors Served

  • Automobile -- precision rubber seals, gaskets, dampers, grommets, bushings for automotive OEMs and Tier 1 suppliers
  • Electronics -- rubber components for electronic devices and assemblies
  • Electrical -- insulating and protective rubber parts for electrical equipment
  • Medical -- mentioned in some directory listings (Maukerja: "automobile, electronic, medical and rubber compound industries")

3. Ownership & Corporate Structure

3.1 Relationship with Mitoyo

Key Finding: Unika Rubber is consistently described as a "joint venture company" with Mitoyo, and Mitoyo's entry is described as a "capital partnership" (資本提携) established in August 1996 -- NOT a wholly-owned subsidiary. The Kuriyama Holdings press release (Feb 14, 2025) lists Mitoyo's Thailand subsidiaries as "great-grandchild companies" (曾孫会社) but does NOT list Unika Rubber in that category, strongly suggesting Mitoyo does NOT hold a controlling majority.

  • Singapore -- immediate neighbor, major electronics/industrial hub
  • Japan -- Mitoyo supply chain (parts for Mitoyo's Japanese customers)
  • Thailand -- Mitoyo's Thai operations may source components
  • Other ASEAN markets -- Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines

Pre-Mitoyo History (1987-1996):
Unika Rubber was founded as a Malaysian-owned company 9 years before Mitoyo's entry. The original founders were Malaysian entrepreneurs (likely Chinese Malaysian, given the Managing Director's name "Ko Chaw Chung"). The company name "Unika" suggests an aspiration for "unique" products. The company was already operational in rubber manufacturing when Mitoyo identified it as a suitable local partner for technology transfer and market access.

11. Recent Developments: Kuriyama Group Acquisition (2025)

11.1 Summary

On February 14, 2025, Kuriyama Holdings (TSE: 3355) announced that its subsidiary Kuriyama Japan would acquire 100% of Mitoyo's shares for ¥6.1 billion. The acquisition was completed on April 2, 2025.

11.2 Seller

11.3 Impact on Unika Rubber

Unika Rubber's partnership stake now indirectly belongs to the Kuriyama Group through Mitoyo. However, since Unika appears to be a JV (not a subsidiary), the operational impact may be limited. Kuriyama's interest is primarily in Mitoyo's automotive parts and hose/pipe businesses. The six stated synergy areas focus on domestic and Thai operations, with no specific mention of Malaysia.
