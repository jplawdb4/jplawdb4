Key Technical Capabilities

Self-Developed Compound Formulations (自社配合): Mitoyo Rubber Thailand develops proprietary rubber compound formulations in-house, enabling customized material development tailored to each customer's specific requirements. This is a core competitive advantage shared with the Malaysia affiliate (Unika Rubber).

Bonding Technologies

  • Metal-to-Rubber Bonding (金属接着): Vulcanization bonding of rubber to metal substrates, used for fan bosses, dampers, and structural rubber-metal composite parts. The parent company describes this as "ゴム+金属の焼付け" (rubber-metal baking/vulcanization bonding).
  • Fabric-to-Rubber Bonding (布接着): Adhesion of rubber to cloth/fabric materials for reinforced diaphragms and specialized sealing components.

Manufacturing Processes

  • Vacuum Compression Molding: Primary process using 200-250 ton vacuum forming presses (12 units). Removes air from mold cavity for higher quality parts with fewer defects.
  • Injection Molding: One 200-ton rubber injection molding machine for complex geometries and high-precision parts.
  • Compound Mixing: In-house rubber compound preparation using 14-18 inch mixing rolls.
  • Secondary Vulcanization: Post-curing in secondary vulcanization chambers (4 units) to achieve final material properties.

Target Industries

Automotive (Primary)

  • Engine components (gaskets, O-rings, seals)
  • Body/chassis parts (grommets, boots, dampers)
  • Interior parts (dampers, rubber-metal composites)
  • HVAC/A/C system components

Motorcycle & Home Appliances

  • Motorcycle engine gaskets & seals
  • Motorcycle suspension components
  • Home appliance sealing & vibration dampening
  • Electronic component seals

10. Major Development: Kuriyama Acquisition (April 2025)

Significant Corporate Change: On April 2, 2025, Kuriyama Japan Co., Ltd. (subsidiary of Kuriyama Holdings, TSE: 3355) acquired 100% of Mitoyo Co., Ltd.'s shares (679,530 shares) for ¥6.1 billion, making Mitoyo a grandchild company of Kuriyama Holdings.

Transaction Details

Announcement DateFebruary 14, 2025
Completion DateApril 2, 2025
Acquisition Price¥6,100 million (excl. advisory fees)
Shares Acquired679,530 shares (100%)
SellerMinori 3-go Investment Partnership (managed by Basic Capital Management)
Investment Period by BCM6 years

Strategic Rationale & Expected Synergies

  1. Passenger Car Market Entry: Kuriyama expands from commercial vehicles into passenger car components via Mitoyo's customer base
  2. Urea SCR Vertical Integration: End-to-end manufacturing from quality sensors (Sun-A) to tanks
  3. Hose Development: Combined expertise targeting construction and agricultural equipment
  4. Geographic Optimization: Western Japan (Kuriyama) + Eastern Japan & Kyushu (Mitoyo)
  5. Asian Manufacturing Expansion: Kuriyama gains access to Mitoyo's Thailand, Malaysia, and China operations
  6. Cross-selling: Bundled industrial materials offerings

Impact on Mitoyo Rubber Thailand

Implications: As part of the Kuriyama Group, Mitoyo Rubber Thailand now has access to a larger corporate group with consolidated revenues of approximately ¥50+ billion. This could mean increased investment capacity, broader customer reach through Kuriyama's agricultural and construction machinery networks, and potential expansion of product lines. Kuriyama's M&A budget includes up to ¥10 billion over 3 years (2025-2027), with the Mitoyo deal consuming ¥6.1 billion of that.

11. Export Markets & Trade

Export Capability

The official DBD business description explicitly includes "นำเข้าและส่งออก" (importing and exporting) as part of Mitoyo Rubber Thailand's registered business activities. The company is registered for both import and export of rubber components, plastic parts, and metal parts.

Likely Export Destinations

  • Japan: Most probable export destination, sending finished rubber parts to Mitoyo Japan or directly to Japanese OEMs/Tier 1 suppliers' Japanese plants
  • ASEAN: Given the parent company's network in Malaysia (Unika) and the broader ASEAN automotive supply chain
  • Regional OEM plants: Japanese automakers operate across ASEAN, and parts may be shipped to assembly plants in Indonesia, Vietnam, Philippines, etc.
Note: Specific export shipment data was not available through free trade databases. The company's relatively small scale (THB ~97M revenue) suggests it primarily serves the domestic Thai market with some export component. The parent company had targeted ¥4 billion in overseas sales by 2020, which was reportedly "already achieved" by 2018, indicating significant intra-group trade activity.
